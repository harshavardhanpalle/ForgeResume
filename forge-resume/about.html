About Forge

Most resume builders
optimize for looking nice.
We optimize for getting read.

Forge started from a simple frustration: a resume can look perfect and still get filtered out before a human ever sees it.

The problem

Templates don't fail people. Mismatched content does.

Most builders hand you a beautiful layout and stop there. They don't tell you that your bullet points don't mention the tools in the job posting, or that your summary buries your strongest qualification three lines down.

Applicant Tracking Systems read resumes before people do. A resume that isn't structured for that first pass never reaches the second one — regardless of how qualified the person is.

Our approach

Score against the real job, not a generic rubric

Forge reads the actual job description you're applying to and compares it against your resume line by line — flagging missing keywords, weak phrasing, and structural gaps that matter for that specific role.

The AI assistant doesn't write your resume for you. It rewrites your own sentences to be sharper, with the credit and the voice staying yours.

Principles

What we optimize for, in order

01

Clarity over decoration

No skill bars, no profile photos by default, no design elements that ATS software can't parse. Every choice has a reason.

02

Specific over generic

"Improved performance" says nothing. "Cut checkout latency by 40%" says everything. We push toward the second every time.

03

Your words, sharpened

The assistant edits what you wrote. It never invents experience or credentials you don't have.
